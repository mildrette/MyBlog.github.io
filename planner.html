Personal Growth
You are not behind. You are building. Every day you try is a win.
Screen & TikTok addiction — break free
Set a screen curfew
No TikTok before 12pm and after 8pm. Put phone in another room. This is not punishment — it is self-respect. Your brain needs rest from constant stimulation.
Replace — don't just stop
You need background noise — that is okay. Replace TikTok with podcasts, Kotlin YouTube tutorials, or music. Same dopamine, different fuel. Same noise, different direction.
Phone-free morning: first 30 minutes
Before any screen — drink water, stretch, say one intention for the day. Even 10 minutes phone-free changes your entire morning mood and your energy for your child.
Track your screen-free wins
Every time you chose something else over TikTok — log it in Notes. Celebrate it. Your brain learns by noticing progress, not just failures.
Depression & learning to sit with quiet
If you feel very low for more than 2 weeks and cannot function — please tell someone. A pastor, a friend, a health worker at Bamenda Regional Hospital. Asking for help is not weakness. It is the bravest thing a mother can do.
Silence is not your enemy
Needing noise is your nervous system protecting you — it learned noise = safe. Start with 2 minutes of quiet while making tea. Then 5. Slowly, your brain learns: silence is safe, not empty.
Movement is medicine — free
A 15-minute walk anywhere in Bamenda changes your brain chemistry. No gym. Just move. It sends your brain the message: "I am still alive and going." Do it daily.
3 things daily — tiny gratitude
Each evening before bed, write 3 things from the day. Not big things. "My son laughed. I drank water. It didn't rain when I walked." Depression shrinks when gratitude grows.
Flexibility & home workout — no gym, no money needed
Morning stretch — 15 min (every day)
Neck rolls → shoulder circles → forward fold → hip opener (legs wide, lean side to side) → cat-cow on hands and knees → child's pose. Do it to music you like. A blanket on the floor is enough.
Strength workout — 3× per week (15 min)
10 squats → 10 push-ups (knees on floor is fine) → 20-second plank → 10 lunges each leg. Repeat 2 rounds. Do it when your son does homework — you both work, together.
Water first — always
Before any meal, drink one glass of water. Reduces emotional hunger, helps digestion, clears the mind. Costs nothing. Your body often sends "hungry" when it means "thirsty."

Affordable weekly meal plan (Bamenda)
Breakfast — every day, rotate
Boiled egg + bread · Corn porridge (akamu) + sugar · Groundnut porridge · Sweet plantain + groundnuts · Rice water + little milk. Goal: protein + carbs every morning. A fed brain learns better at school.
Lunch — school days
Rice + beans + egg or small fish · Fufu + eru / vegetable soup · Plantains + beans. Pack water — no sweet drinks. Sugar drinks at school reduce attention and energy by afternoon.
Dinner — light
Boiled yam or cocoyam + soup · Pasta + sardine + tomato sauce · Beans + fried plantain. Include vegetables in at least 2 meals every day.
Snacks
Groundnuts (best brain food, very cheap) · Banana · Boiled corn · Bread. Avoid daily sachets of sweet water — water is always better for a 7-year-old brain.
At 7 he needs: iron (beans, dark leafy veg like eru/ndole), protein (eggs, fish, groundnuts, beans), and complex carbs for energy. Groundnuts are one of the best and cheapest brain foods in Cameroon.

Daily parenting checklist
How to raise a strong 7-year-old
Listen, really listen
When he talks, put your phone down. Look at his face. Respond. A child who feels heard grows up with confidence and emotional health. 5 minutes of full attention beats 2 hours of distracted presence.
Routine is safety for children
Same wake time, meal times, bedtime. Children with predictable days feel safer and behave better. Start with bedtime: 8:30pm every night. This one change will transform your mornings.
Praise effort, not results
Say "I saw how hard you tried" not just "you are smart." Children praised for effort become more resilient. They don't give up when things are hard — and things will be hard.
Limits with love
"No" is a complete sentence. But explain why: "No more screen because your brain needs rest to grow." Rules with reasons build respect, not resentment.
You are his first teacher
He is always watching you. When you put your phone down, he learns to do the same. When you say sorry after a mistake, he learns accountability. You are already enough. Keep going.
